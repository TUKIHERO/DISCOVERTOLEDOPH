THE HINULAWAN FESTIVAL

The Hinulawan Festival stands as the grandest and most vibrant celebration in Toledo City, Cebu Province. This festivity encompasses a rich tapestry of traditional dances, communal feasting, and various exciting elements.

According to local lore, the term 'hinulawan' originates from 'hin' for 'hinaguan' (fruits of labor) and 'ulawan' for 'bulawan' (meaning gold).

Toledo City received its name, transitioning from Old Hinulawan (now Daang Lungsod) to New Hinulawan, now known as Toledo. Established by influential leaders, including Fernando Poloyapoy, the city's name was bestowed by the Spaniards, echoing a town in Spain called Toledo.

The Hinulawan Festival is a distinctive annual religious celebration, expressing gratitude to the city's patron, Saint John of Sahagun. Celebrated on the 12th of June, it marks the pinnacle of festivities that unfold in the preceding days.
