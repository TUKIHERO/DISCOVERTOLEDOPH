PLACES

LAPOS-LAPOS CAVE

Ideal for adventurers, this cave showcases remarkable rock formations, adorned with impressive stalactites and stalagmites.

Beyond its cliffs, the cave unveils another spot where you can immerse yourself in a breathtaking view of the Copper City.

SAN JUAN DE SAHAGUN PARISH
ARCHDIOCESAN SHRINE

Toledo was officially founded as a town in 1861, originally bearing the name Hinulawan, alternatively spelled Jinulauan.

The Toledo Church attained parish status on July 8, 1863, with construction commencing in 1951.

ILIHAN BRIDGE

While not officially designated as a tourist attraction, many individuals still consider this man-made marvel worth a visit.

Constructed with the primary goal of alleviating traffic issues in Toledo City, it serves as a compelling reminder of mankind's capability to create remarkable structures.

BIGA PIT

At first glance, one might mistake it for the Grand Canyon, but this hidden gem is nestled in Toledo. Despite technically being a wasteland,

visits are permitted for educational purposes or private tours, granted permission is obtained from the mining company.

MT. TAGAYTAY

Toledo has its own peak to boast about Tagaytay Hill or Mount Tagaytay. Despite being underrated,

trekkers are generously rewarded with a beautiful view of Toledo upon reaching the highest point.

LA VUE CABINE

Perched atop a mountain, La Vue Cabine is the ideal setting for a long-awaited overnight reunion with your barkada.

This exclusive wood cabin offers a grander perspective, allowing you to behold the scenic beauty of Toledo.
