BARANGAY AWIHAO

Awihao is a barangay located in Toledo's midland. Home to 3,536 residents, it measures 2.09 square kilometers.
It is accessible from the national highway via two roads, namely, the Bato-Awihao Road and Awihao-Cabito-onan Road. According to tradition, this place got its name from a huge and imposing tree that the residents named awihao.
It was said to be meters away from a well that is still there today. This barangay is noted as the "egg-basket" of the city. Every Sunday, its poultry farms provide eggs to Toledo's market every Sunday, and even supply Cebu City. Besides poultry farming, the residents of Awihao also plant coconuts, cereals, and root crops. Some continue to sell dried tobacco leaves,
a reminder that this place was once a tobacco plantation in the olden days. but serve as floats in the fishermen's nets. The trees also protect the residents' houses from strong winds.

BARANGAY CALONG-CALONG

Calongcalong. This is the smallest barangay in the city, with a land area of 0.56 square kilometer.
It has a population of 1,118 and an intercensal growth of -6.44%.

BARANGAY CAMBANG-UG

Cambang-ug. The barangay has a population of 3,414. Its land area measures 8.05 square kilometers.
